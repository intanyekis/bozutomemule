The Meaning Behind The Song: Meciendo by Carlos Guastavino

Song Information

MeciendoCarlos GuastavinoCarlos Guastavino & Gabriela MistralSeis canciones de cuna (1943)N/APopN/A

Carlos Guastavino’s song “Meciendo” is a beautiful and haunting lullaby that has captivated audiences since its release in 1943. The lyrics, written by Carlos Guastavino himself in collaboration with the renowned poet Gabriela Mistral, delve into the deep meaning of comforting and nurturing a child.

“Meciendo” translates to “rocking” in English, and the song portrays the act of rocking a child to sleep with gentle and soothing words. The lyrics depict the natural forces of the sea, wind, and the divine as they create a serene and peaceful atmosphere for the child to rest.

In the first verse, the sea is described as rocking with its thousands of waves, creating a divine sensation. The mares, symbolizing the lovers of the sea, also partake in the rocking motion, creating a nurturing environment for the child.

The second verse introduces the wind as a wandering entity in the night, rocking the fields of wheat. Just like the sea, the wind has its own lovers, and their presence adds to the protective and nurturing feeling that envelops the child.

The final verse introduces the concept of God, portrayed as a father figure who silently rocks his thousands of worlds. The hand of God, even in the shadows, can be felt, creating a sense of security as the child is gently rocked.

The collaboration between Carlos Guastavino and Gabriela Mistral in creating these poetic and profound lyrics adds an extra layer of depth to the song. Both artists are renowned in their respective fields, and their combined talents have truly brought “Meciendo” to life.

Personally, “Meciendo” holds a special place in my heart. Growing up, my mother would sing this lullaby to me every night as she rocked me to sleep. The melody and the tender words never failed to soothe me and create a sense of comfort and safety. The song became a cherished part of my childhood, and it continues to evoke warm memories and emotions whenever I hear it.

The gentle melody composed by Carlos Guastavino perfectly complements the gentle and nurturing message of the lyrics. The simplicity of the song, both musically and lyrically, enhances its beauty and impact. The repetitive nature of the lyrics mirrors the motion of rocking, creating a calming and mesmerizing effect.

“Meciendo” serves as a reminder of the universal experience of being rocked to sleep as a child. It evokes a sense of nostalgia and reminds us of the love and care we received during our early years. It’s a song that transcends language barriers and touches the hearts of listeners from different cultures and backgrounds.

Whether you understand the lyrics or not, the emotions conveyed by “Meciendo” are universally relatable. It speaks to the tender bond between a caregiver and a child, and the desire to provide comfort and security. It reminds us of the power of music to create a deep and lasting connection.

In conclusion, “Meciendo” by Carlos Guastavino is a timeless lullaby that beautifully captures the essence of rocking a child to sleep. The song’s poetic lyrics, combined with the gentle melody, create an enchanting and soothing experience for both the listener and the child being rocked. It’s a song that holds a special place in the hearts of many, including my own, and continues to bring comfort and joy to those who listen.
