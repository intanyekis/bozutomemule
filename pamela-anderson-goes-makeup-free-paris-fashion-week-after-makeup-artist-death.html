Pamela Anderson Ditches Makeup At Paris Fashion Week, Insists Its Better For Me

Posted by Martina Birk on Wednesday, July 17, 2024

Highlights

  • Pamela Anderson embraced a fresh-faced look at Paris Fashion Week, showcasing her natural beauty at high-profile events.
  • Designer Victoria Beckham paid tribute to Pamela on Instagram and many comments praised her stunning appearance, encouraging more collaboration.
  • Pamela's documentary, released earlier this year, helped her reclaim control over her narrative and she now chooses to go makeup-free in honor of her late makeup artist.

Pamela Anderson looked fresh-faced at Paris Fashion Week over the weekend, as she stepped out completely makeup-free.

The former Baywatch appearance made several public appearances for the international celebration of fashion. On Friday, September 29, the actress showed up to the Victoria Beckham Womenswear Spring-Summer 2024 wearing a delicate floral dress.

The light green and yellow hues complimented Pamela’s hair, which she wore down. True to her signature style in recent years, Pamela sported a makeup-free face for the glam event.

Following the fashion show, designer Victoria Beckham paid tribute to Pamela in a sweet Instagram post marking the event, which included shots of other famous faces who attended the event, including Kim Kardashian and Eva Longoria. “So many kisses to my #VBMuses for coming to my show xx @KimKardashian @IsabelaGrutman @EvaLongoria @PamelaAnderson @ShaninamShaik @KrisJenner,” Victoria wrote in the caption.

Pamela’s Documentary Helped Her Come Back To The Spotlight

Many of the comments remarked at how stunning Pamela Anderson looked at the fashion show and encouraged Victoria to showcase more of the actress in her designs moving forward.

The following day, on Saturday, September 30, Pamela was seen at the Vivienne Westwood SS2024 show. Upon leaving the venue, the actress was photographed in an oversized brown trench coat with a matching hat that was equally large, providing for an incognito look. But the television personality’s flawless skin was still visible as she smiled for flashing cameras.

For many years, Pamela kept a low-profile from the public following her rise to fame in the ‘80s. However, she took control of the media’s narrative over her life, including the release of her documentary Pamela, a Love Story, which debuted on Netflix earlier this year with the help of her son, Brandon, who acted as an executive producer.

“I wasn’t necessarily out to make a film that made her look her best. I was just going to tell the truth,” Brandon said of making the film.

“People will come up to her and say things like, ‘I used to not like you, I used to think you did all this stuff for fame and personal gain, but I like you. You’re a real person,’” he went on. “To know that a film, telling a story, can have a real impact on someone’s life, that’s the real payoff.”

The Death Of Pamela’s Makeup Artist Pushed Her To Ditch Makeup

Now, Pamela has been more in the public face than ever recently ever since the documentary made waves – as evidenced by Pamela’s recent sighting at Paris Fashion Week. But the actress is doing things on her terms, including forgoing makeup when she so feels like it.

Though she was previously known for her thick liner and dark lips in her youth, Pamela admitted to Elle earlier this year that wasn’t necessarily the look she wanted to go for. “I just went along with what people were telling me what to do,” she explained.

Moreover, Pamela revealed she’s started wearing less and less makeup since her beloved makeup artist and friend died from breast cancer in 2019. “She was the best,” Pamela went on. “And since then, I just felt, without Alexis, it’s just better for me not to wear makeup.” She called her decision, “freeing, and fun, and a little rebellious too.”
