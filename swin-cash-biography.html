Who is Swin Cash?

Swin Cash is an American retired professional, very hot, and stunning basketball player.

She is also a prolific scorer and rebounder, as well as a capable ball-handler and defender who helped to lead the University of Connecticut women’s basketball team to national titles in 2000 and 2002.

Swin Cash: Birth facts, Family, Childhood

Swin Cash was born to her parents Cynthia Cash and Clifford C. Hunt in McKeesport, Pennsylvania, in the United States on 22 September 1979.

She has got one sister and two brothers. She was raised in her birthplace and properly cared for by her mother along with her one sister and two brothers.

In which school did Swin Cash attend?

Swin studied at McKeesport Area High School at McKeesport. She played the basketball game and was popular for her playing skills, as well as her participation in the WBCA High School All-America Game.

She attended the University of Connecticut where she became a member of the inaugural class (2006) of inductees to the University of Connecticut women’s basketball “Huskies of Honor” recognition program.

Swin Cash: Profession, Carrer

Cash was selected by the Women’s National Basketball Association, Detroit Shock in the 2002 WNBA Draft, second overall after university graduation.

For the first time, she led the Detroit Shock to the WNBA Championship title in 2003 and won the gold medal with the U.S. women’s basketball team at the 2004 Olympic Games.

Cash worked as a studio analyst on ESPN’s NBA Fastbreak (according to ESPN.com) in the offseason and she coordinated with Teresa Edwards in presenting in-game commentary for NBC’s presentation of the women’s basketball tournament from the network’s New York broadcast studios during the 2008 Beijing Olympic games.

Swin also acted in the movie Bring It On: All or Nothing. Cash got traded to the Seattle Storm for the draft rights of the No. 4 picks in the 2008 WNBA draft on February 19, 2008.

Cash along with Sue Bird and Lauren Jackson won their second championship in 2010. While helping the Detroit Shock win the WNBA championship, Cash suffered from a herniated disk for more than two years in 2006.

She came back to lead the Western Conference over the Eastern Conference at Mohegan Sun in Uncasville, Connecticut in 2009 and led all scorers with an All-Star record of 22 points in a 130-118 win.

Swin Cash got retired on June 7, 2016, after achieving grand success in the sector of Basketball.

Lifetime Achievement, Awards

Swin won the WBCA All-American award in 1998. Her team was awarded as a Big East Third Team in 2000. She was awarded the title of Most Outstanding Player of the NCAA Basketball Tournament in 2002.

Further, she won in the WNBA All-Star selection in 2009. She also won Sears Shooting Stars Championship four times over the years, 2007, 2013, 2014, and 2015.

She also was awarded the Honorary Doctorate of Public Service from Washington and Jefferson College in 2011.
