McDonald's Latest Celebrity Crossover: BTS Meal Coming In May

Posted by Martina Birk on Sunday, June 23, 2024

In a rather unexpected chain of events, lovers of the billion dollar food chain and fans of KPOP alike rejoice as McDonald's announced it's newest collaboration with hit Korean supergroup BTS. After the craze of the Travis Scott burger made the company millions, it's clear that McDonald's has it's eye laser focused on racking in even more from this iconic team up.

The 'BTS Meal' comes with "a 10-piece Chicken McNuggets, medium fries and Coke as well as the US debut of two new dipping sauces," according to CNN Business, as was announced via McDonald's Twitter. McDonald's replied to their own tweet with a list indicating when countries across the world could see the order on their local drive thru menus, along with a link to their Tik Tok video captioned, "ba da ba ba ba but make it BTS."

RELATED: K-Pop Superstars 'BTS' Secret To Making Millions Of Dollars, Explained

While the meal may seem no different than a normal order one could make at McDonald's, what sets this promotion apart is its addition of new sauces, Sweet Chili and Cajun. These sauces originated in the chain's South Korean locations and will hit the US market for the first time with the 'BTS meal'.

The meal is set to hit the States - where sales have risen 5.5% particularly from promotions - on May 26th, and with eleven other countries such as Austria, the Dominican Republic, and the band's homeland of Korea . The following day thirteen more countries will add the BTS meal to their menu and so on until it's available in 50 countries by June 26th.

"BTS truly lights up the world stage, uniting people across the globe through their music," says the Chief Marketing Officer of McDonald's US division, Morgan Flatley in a statement, "We're excited to bring customers even closer to their beloved band in a way only McDonald's can – through our delicious food – when we introduce the BTS signature order on our menu next month."

The band's management shared similar sentiments with a representative from their record label BIGHIT MUSIC saying, "The band has great memories with McDonald's. We're excited about this collaboration and can't wait to share the BTS Meal with the world."

With BTS's enormous success across and notoriety across the world, it's quite clear why McDonald's took to the group to promote their food. The group toppled the likes of American household names like Taylor Swift and Beyonce in global sales. With their recent Grammy nomination  for their hit song "Dynamite", these boys are quickly becoming a force to be reckoned with in the music industry. There's no doubt that the new  'BTS Meal' will bring the boy band and the food chain millions in the coming months.
