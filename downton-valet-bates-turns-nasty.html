Downton valet Bates turns nasty

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Saturday, July 27, 2024

Brendan Coyle’s first post-“Downton Abbey” role is a far cry from the mild-mannered valet John Bates.

In the new Esquire Network dramedy “Spotless” (premiering Saturday at 10 p.m.), the British actor plays organized crime leader Nelson Clay, a sophisticated, self-made multimillionaire who has a hand in everything from prostitution to drugs to protection rackets in modern-day London.

“That’s generally an actor’s instinct, once you’ve finished a long job you want to do something different,” Coyle tells The Post. “I’m a character actor so you want to show your range.”

The 10-episode series (which originally aired on Canal+ in France last spring) centers on Jean Bastiere (Marc-André Grondin), who runs a crime-scene-cleaning business and has a seemingly perfect family life. But when his estranged, crook brother Martin (Denis Ménochet) crashes into his life, Jean must clean up his mess — which entangles both in Clay’s criminal web.

“This drug deal goes wrong and two of my henchman meet [Jean] and … here’s a very brutal and violent scene in the first episode,” Coyle says. “My boys come back to me and say ‘This guy is a genius, he can clean up anything, he can make it look like suicide, make anything look like anything.’

“I think ‘better have him on our team.’ I make him an offer he can’t refuse to perform acts of his skill at my behest.”

The fact that Clay rarely gets his own hands dirty made the role appealing to Coyle, with his character ruling a criminal empire with an almost invisible hand.

“The menace of the quiet man is much more threatening than the people who shout and stomp and beat around with a baseball bat,” he says.

While “Spotless” filmed in London, it may be the last thing Coyle shoots in England for a while. He’s moving to LA in January to build a career in Hollywood — just in time for the US premiere of “Downton Abbey” (Jan. 3 on PBS).

The sixth and final season of the period drama picks up in 1924, and fans should be pleased to hear that Mr. Bates and Anna (Joanne Froggatt) have finally put that dreadful Mr. Green saga behind them.

“We get rid of that very quickly,” Coyle says of the murder accusation that has dogged the characters for the past two seasons.

“They’ve had a rough and rocky road but we get to see John Bates smile more in this [season]. I still don’t have any jokes or songs, but he’s much more content.
“I think it’s our most opulent [season]. It’s very grand,” he adds.

“There are some troubled storylines and there are some very happy endings.”
