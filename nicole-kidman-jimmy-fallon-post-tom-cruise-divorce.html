Following Her Divorce From Tom Cruise, Nicole Kidman Admits This Celeb Failed His Attempt To Ask For

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Saturday, August 17, 2024

Highlights

  • Nicole Kidman had a big crush on Jimmy Fallon, but he missed the chance to ask for her number not once, but twice!
  • Kidman's awkward date with Fallon ended with no chemistry, making her think he was uninterested or possibly gay.
  • Fortunately, Kidman moved on from Fallon's rejection and went on to date rapper Q-Tip and ultimately married Keith Urban in 2006.

During the 90s, Nicole Kidman and Tom Cruise were one of Hollywood’s biggest “it couples.” The two first met while working together on the 1990 film Days of Thunder. That same year, they tied the knot in an extremely private ceremony. Kidman and Cruise adopted their children, Isabella "Bella" Jane and Connor Anthony in 1992 and 1995, respectively, and worked together in two more movies before they ultimately split up in 2001.

After a long-drawn and incredibly dramatic divorce, both actors went on with their lives. During the early 2000s, Kidman was romantically linked to many celebrities, including a secret engagement with Lenny Kravitz in 2003. Most recently, Kidman revealed that she had the biggest crush on a comedian, but she quickly lost interest after an awkward date and a failed attempt to ask for her number.

Nicole Kidman Was Disappointed That Jimmy Fallon Never Asked For Her Number

As it turns out, Jimmy Fallon blew his chances of dating Nicole Kidman not once but twice! During another appearance on The Tonight Show, Kidman revealed that Fallon had a second chance to ask her out but completely blew it off.

“We were at David Fincher’s house,” the Big Little Lies star recounted. “It was when Brad and Jen were still together, so it was a long time ago. And you could’ve asked for my number then cause it was round two, well, second chance. And you still didn’t ask for it!”

For a moment, Fallon couldn’t seem to remember their second meeting, so Kidman went on to explain what happened. “My agent had said, ‘Jimmy’s gonna be there and you’ll see he can ask for your number,’” Kidman recalled. “We were lurking in the kitchen and everyone and everyone went out and I’m, like, waiting and waiting and waiting, and you’re at the buffet. And this is so true and you know it! And you still didn’t ask for my number.” Kidman was clearly upset about this.

However, Fallon was completely shocked by the revelation. He revealed that he was oblivious to the fact that Kidman had a crush on him and never intended to reject her. He never thought he stood a chance with her.

“So 2002, I'm single, but I don't think about this remotely, my chances of dating [her], I think she was named most beautiful person that year! So this is beyond my understanding,” Fallon revealed in an interview.

Nicole Kidman Had The Weirdest First Date With Jimmy Fallon

A couple of years after her divorce from Tom Cruise, Nicole Kidman decided to give love another chance. The actress briefly dated and got engaged with Lenny Kravitz, but broke things off with the musician in 2003. Shortly after, she tried to pursue a popular comedian, but things didn’t go as planned.

In a 2015 interview for The Tonight Show with Jimmy Fallon, Kidman admitted that she used to have a major crush on the comedian. The two met through a mutual friend, who tried to set them up. “Rick, our mutual friend, was like 'Jimmy wants to meet you. You can go over to his apartment,’” Nicole Kidman explained of his first meeting with Jimmy Fallon. Since she was single at the time, she decided to throw her shot and agreed to go to his place, thinking it would be a date.

However, Fallon had completely different expectations of their first meeting. According to the late-night talk show host, when his friend Rick called to tell him he was bringing Kidman over to meet at his apartment, Fallon thought it would be a business meeting.

“I thought [she'd come over] for this movie she was doing, Bewitched, which Will Ferrell ended up getting [...] I thought it was a chemistry thing,” Fallon told Deadline, admitting that he felt completely blindsided by Kidman’s revelation, as it never occurred to him that they were on a date.

Unfortunately, what could’ve been a date ended up being an awkward experience for both celebrities. “So I go over there, and you're there in a baseball cap and nothing, and you wouldn't talk. You didn't say anything,” Kidman told Fallon.

“And then you put a video game on or something, and I'm like, ‘This is so bad.’ And you didn't talk at all. So after about an hour and a half, I thought, ‘He has no interest, this is so embarrassing.’ And I left and went, ‘OK, no chemistry,’ and I went, ‘Maybe he's gay.’”

Nicole Kidman Quickly Moved On From Jimmy Fallon’s Rejection

Nicole Kidman quickly moved on from Jimmy Fallon’s unintentional rejection. In 2003, the actress reportedly dated rapper Q-Tip after co-starring with him in the 2002 science-fiction comedy movie, The Complex. Around this time, the actress was also linked to Lenny Kravitz. Kidman not only admitted to dating the musician but has also revealed that they got engaged but decided to call it off before the wedding.

Over the years, Kidman has been linked to celebrities such as Robbie Williams, Jude Law, and Steve Bing. However, she’s been happily married to Keith Urban since 2006. The actress met the musician at G'Day LA, an event honoring Australians in America, and decided to get married after less than a year together. The two now have two daughters, Sunday Rose and Faith Margaret.
