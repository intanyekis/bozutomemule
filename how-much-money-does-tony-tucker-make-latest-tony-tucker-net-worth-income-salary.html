Tony Craig Tucker (born December 27, 1958) is a former professional heavyweight boxer who competed in the United States. In his professional fighting career, Tucker competed from 1980 until 1998. He was known as “TNT” and earned a gold medal in the 1979 Pan American Games. He also has the dubious distinction of being the world heavyweight champion for the shortest period of time in history, having reigned for just 64 days. Tucker had a distinguished amateur career, culminating in his victory in the 1979 National AAU Light Heavyweight Championship. In addition, he won the Pan-American Games and the World Cup in Light Heavyweight in 1979. Additionally, he lost a decision to LeRoy Murphy in the finals of the 1977 Ohio State Fair competition, and he was defeated by Alex DeLucia in the finals of the Western Olympic trials in 1980.
